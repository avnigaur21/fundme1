Understand the pathways to become a recognized incubator, TBI, or eligible institution within the startup ecosystem.

TBI Recognition

Focused on technology-intensive startups, Technology Business Incubators (TBIs) provide specialized infrastructure and lab access. Recognition is typically provided by DST or MeitY.

FOR: Engineering Colleges & R&D Units
Incubator Accreditation

General startup incubators that provide physical space, mentorship, and Seed Fund support (e.g., SISFS). Evaluated based on network strength and sectoral focus.

FOR: Private Entities & Institutional Labs

Pathway to TBI Recognition

A typical roadmap for an institution to achieve official TBI status.

1
Institutional Eligibility
Must be a host institute (HI) like a University, Engineering College, or R&D institution with at least 5 years of history.
2
Infrastructure Readiness
Allocation of dedicated physical space (~10,000 sq ft) and specialized lab equipment for startups.
3
Governance & Team
Formation of a dedicated Section 8 company and appointment of a full-time CEO and management team.
4
Application & Evaluation
Submission of detailed project report (DPR) followed by initial screening and committee evaluation.
5
Site Review & Recognition
Physical site visit by subject matter experts to verify readiness, followed by final approval and licensing.

Typical Requirements

  • Registered Legal Entity (Section 8 Company)
  • Dedicated CEO and full-time ops team
  • Evidence of R&D or startup past support
  • Financial audit reports for last 3 years

Timeline Expectations

Phase 1: Preparation 1–2 Months
Phase 2: Submission & Screen ~1 Month
Phase 3: Committee Review 2–3 Months

Note: Timelines vary by ministry and sectoral focus.
